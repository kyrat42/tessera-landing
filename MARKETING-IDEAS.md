# Tessera — Marketing Ideas & Survey Quotes

> Last updated: 2026-07-10
> Source: ongoing beta tester survey (see Tessera app repo's beta testing effort)
> Purpose: raw material for landing page copy, positioning, and testimonials — not a task tracker. See the Tessera app repo's `BACKLOG.md` for actionable product bugs/features pulled from this same survey.

---

## Key Takeaways (as of 5 respondents)

**Sample size caveat first**: 5 respondents, and only ~3 gave substantive answers across most questions (Respondent 1 churned early, Respondent 5 answered one question). Treat everything below as directional — good for prioritizing what to test next, not a mandate to rewrite the page.

**High confidence — cross-validated by multiple independent respondents:**
1. **"Scattered" is the #1 pain point.** Independently named 4 times by 2+ different respondents ("centralize," "jumbled together," "scattered across different apps," and a genuine before/after: "scattered notes... → a more structured way"). The "centralize everything in one place" pitch is close to confirmed, not speculative.
2. **The areas-of-life/mosaic concept matches how people already think about their lives**, unprompted — one respondent's own life naturally decomposed into work/household/errands/personal, another explicitly compared it to their existing multi-calendar Google setup ("fits really well into your areas of life").
3. **Schedule flexibility is a real, recurring need** — two respondents, two different flavors (self-directed flexible professional vs. externally-imposed shift work). Validates "Routine without rigidity" for more than one persona.
4. **Prioritization difficulty is real friction Tessera resolves** — two respondents named it before using the app; validates the Priority Levels feature specifically.

**Medium confidence — one respondent so far, worth watching for repetition before acting:**
5. "External memory / never lose track of your day" — a distinct angle nothing in current copy addresses.
6. "Sense of accomplishment" from progress-tracking (Weekly Hours) — resonates as an emotional payoff, not just a time-tracking utility, in two respondents' own words.
7. The recent onboarding rework appears to be working — one respondent who'd previously churned over "confusing setup" credited a since-shipped update with fixing what blocked them (recurring tasks / multi-day blocks specifically).

**Explicitly unconfirmed — don't act on this yet:**
8. Neurodivergence as a target segment (3 of 5 respondents) is confounded by recruitment channel (sourced partly from an autism community) and survey design (explicitly asks about it). Real theory, not yet evidence.

**Gaps surfaced, not yet in copy:**
9. The "To Schedule" reminder-resurfacing feature solves a named pain point ("forget less urgent responsibilities") but isn't mentioned anywhere on the landing page.

## User Segments & Target Market Trends

Tracks respondent profiles and engagement level, so we can tell target-market signal apart from edge-case feedback. Only trust persona/positioning conclusions drawn from respondents marked **Representative** below.

> **Methodology caveat on the neurodivergence pattern (3 of 4 respondents so far):** this survey pool is not a random/organic sample with respect to neurodivergence. Two compounding selection effects are in play — (1) at least part of the beta tester pool was recruited by promoting the app directly to an autism-specific community, and (2) the survey itself explicitly asks about neurodivergence, which primes respondents to mention it. Both inflate the apparent rate versus what an unprompted, general-audience survey would show. **Treat "neurodivergent users respond well to Tessera" as an open theory to keep testing, not a confirmed target-market finding**, until it shows up from respondents *not* sourced via the autism-group channel, or gets corroborated some other way (e.g. app store reviews, organic word-of-mouth mentions). Don't build landing page copy around this segment on current evidence.

## Data quality flags

Responses noted here should **not** be used as marketing signal — logged for transparency only.

- **Respondent 3**, "what would need to change for you to use Tessera daily" — answer was generic app-review boilerplate ("seamless part of my routine," "compelling reason to return each day," "rewards for consistent engagement") that contradicts their own reported multiple-times-daily usage. Reads as autopilot/stock/possibly AI-generated rather than a genuine reflection on their actual habits with this app.
- **Respondent 3**, "what made you sign up" — same pattern repeats: "curious about the platform," "something useful and personalized," "fit naturally into my daily routine." Generic enough to apply to any app; no Tessera-specific detail (contrast with Respondent 2, who named concrete specifics like "jumbled together" and "remember the details of the day").
- **Status**: two boilerplate-style answers in a row is enough to downgrade this whole respondent's data from representative to uncertain (reflected in the segments table above). Their remaining answers should still be read, but treat any that stay this generic as non-signal; only a genuinely specific, Tessera-particular answer should be pulled back into the main themes table.

## Strongest cross-respondent theme so far: "scattered"

Independently mentioned **4 times across 2 respondents**, in different words each time:
- Respondent 1: wanted to "centralize" routines and tasks
- Respondent 2: "everything is jumbled together"
- Respondent 3: "everything felt scattered across different apps" (before Tessera)
- Respondent 3 again: "scattered notes, calendar reminders, and to-do lists" → "a more structured way" (explicitly resolved by Tessera)

This is no longer a single quote to consider — it's the most-validated pain point in the survey, and it crosses segments (not just the neurodivergent-linked responses). The last mention is a genuine before/after testimonial, which makes the "centralize everything in one place" pitch idea below close to confirmed rather than speculative, and gives us usable problem→resolution copy, not just a problem statement.

## Open ideas to explore in copy
- **"Centralize everything in one place" pitch, alongside the existing "balance/mosaic" framing — now backed by 3 independent mentions of the "scattered" pain point (see above). This is the highest-confidence copy idea in this doc so far.**
- Lean into the emotional "before" state (frustration, lack of rest) more explicitly before introducing the mosaic concept.
- Consider surfacing the checklist/completion UX visually as its own selling point, not just implied through screenshots.
- "External memory" / "never lose track of your day" angle — unaddressed by current copy, surfaced by an active daily-use respondent (see segments table). Watch for repetition before elevating to a headline.
- Possible gap: the "To Schedule" reminder-resurfacing feature (incomplete tasks from yesterday reappear on Home) directly solves a named pain point ("forget less urgent responsibilities") but isn't mentioned anywhere in current landing page copy.
